Learning Objectives

  • Predict how charge sign changes the direction of magnetic deflection.
  • Explain why magnetic force changes direction but not speed.
  • Use F = |q|vB, r = mv / |qB|, and f = |qB| / 2πm to connect numbers with motion.
  • Compare simulated motion with the right-hand rule.

Theory

A moving charged particle in a magnetic field experiences the Lorentz magnetic force, F = q(v x B). In this lab the velocity is drawn in the screen plane and the magnetic field points into or out of the screen.

Because the force is perpendicular to velocity, it acts as a centripetal force. That gives circular motion when q and B are nonzero: r = mv / |qB|.

The angular speed is omega = qB / m, and the cyclotron frequency shown here is f = |qB| / 2πm.

Student Challenge Questions

  1. Set q = +1 and B = +2. Which way does the particle bend? Now change q to -1. What reverses?
  2. Keep q and B fixed. Double the mass. What happens to the measured radius?
  3. Increase velocity from 4 to 8. Does force increase, radius increase, or both?
  4. Find two different settings that produce the same radius. Record your values.
  5. Set q = 0. Explain why the magnetic field symbols are still visible but the path is straight.

Teacher Worksheet

  1. Warm-up: Ask students to predict the path before pressing Reset.
  2. Guided test: q = +2, B = +2, v = 6, m = 24. Students calculate F, r, and f, then compare with readouts.
  3. Concept check: Ask why magnetic force cannot increase the particle speed in this setup.
  4. Extension: Ask students to export the CSV trail data and plot x versus y in a spreadsheet.
  5. Exit ticket: One sentence explaining how mass affects curvature.

What to Notice

The force is always at right angles to the velocity, so the particle turns instead of speeding up.

When q or B is zero, there is no magnetic force and the trail becomes a straight line.
